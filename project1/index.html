I'm a second year Game Design & Development major who likes coding, playing games and talking with friends.

I do a lot of work in Unity and Monogame so I have really strong C# skills. A majority of my programming experience came from creating mods for the MMO Realm of the Mad God. I'm currently working on creating a client port to Unity since the old one was made in Flash.

I've always liked puzzles and video games so when I started learning to code in 2016 I immediately loved the problem solving that came with being able to finally make video games.

For my career I'm still not sure whether I want to work at an Indie studio or a game industry. Both have their own pros and cons. Modding on a small team allowed me to be really close with the player base as a developer, and reading their enjoyment and getting feedback about something I made is the best thing.
